Introduction

This chapter also acts as a bridge between class 10 and 11. First of all we will learn some terminologies that we have already studied in class 10.
In our day to day surrounding we talks about the two types of quantities.

1.Non-Physical quantities:
The quantities that cannot be measured directly or indirectly are known as non-physical quantities. Eg: love,hate,etc.

2. Physical quantities:
The quantities that can be measured directly and indirectly is known as physical quantities.
They are of two types:
a. Fundamental Quantities: The quantities that have its own identity and do not depends upon other are called fundamental quantities.
The fundamental quantities are listed below:

S.N. Fundamental quantities SI unit Dimension
1. Length Meter [L]
2. Mass Kilogram [Kg]
3. Time Second [T]
4. Luminious Intensity Candela [Cd]
5. Amount of the substance Mole [Mol]
6. Temperature Kelvin [k]
7. Electric Current Amphere [A]

[NOTE: Among seven different fundamental quantities, mass,length,time are considered to be the basic quantities because they are mostly used in creating the derived quantities than other fundamental quantities.]
